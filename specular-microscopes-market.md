<p><ul><li>Contact Specular Microscopes</li><li>Non-contact Specular Microscopes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The specular microscopes market consists of two primary types: contact and non-contact specular microscopes. Contact specular microscopes involve direct contact with the sample, allowing for high-resolution imaging and detailed analysis of cellular structures, often used in ophthalmology for corneal evaluations. In contrast, non-contact specular microscopes operate without touching the sample, minimizing the risk of contamination and injury. This type is beneficial for examining delicate surfaces and is commonly utilized in research and industrial applications to assess material properties and quality.</p></p>
