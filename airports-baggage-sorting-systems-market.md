<p><p>Airports use various baggage sorting systems to enhance efficiency in handling passenger luggage. Tilt-tray systems utilize trays that tilt to redirect bags to specific destinations, suitable for high-speed sorting and accommodating diverse baggage sizes. Cross-belt systems use a network of parallel belts to transport bags, offering quick sorting and minimal damage risk. Both technologies improve the speed and accuracy of baggage handling, crucial for managing increasing passenger volumes and ensuring timely aircraft departures in modern airports.</p></p>
