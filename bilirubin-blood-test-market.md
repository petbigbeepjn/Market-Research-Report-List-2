<p><p>The bilirubin blood test market comprises two primary types: transcutaneous and bench-top testing. Transcutaneous bilirubin meters non-invasively measure bilirubin levels through the skin, making them particularly suitable for infant care by reducing discomfort. In contrast, bench-top bilirubin testing involves laboratory-based analysis using blood samples for more accurate and comprehensive results. Both methods cater to varying clinical needs, with transcutaneous testing offering convenience and speed, while bench-top approaches provide detailed analysis crucial for diagnosing liver and hemolytic conditions.</p></p>
